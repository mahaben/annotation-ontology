<b>Please read this carefully: Specification is undergoing changes.</b><br />
The Annotation Ontology specification is currently used as input for the activities of the <a href='http://www.w3.org/community/openannotation/'>W3C Open Annotation Community Group</a> that works towards a common, RDF-based, specification for annotating digital resources. The Group effort starts by working towards a reconciliation of two proposals that have emerged over the past two years: the <a href='http://code.google.com/p/annotation-ontology/'>Annotation Ontology </a> and the <a href='http://www.openannotation.org/spec/beta/'>Open Annotation Model</a>. Initially, editors of these proposals will closely collaborate to devise a common draft specification that addresses requirements and use cases that were identified in the course of their respective efforts. The goal is to make this draft available for public feedback and experimentation in the second quarter of 2012. The final deliverable of the Open Annotation Community Group will be a specification, published under an appropriate open license, that is informed by the existing proposals, the common draft specification, and the community feedback.

<b>AO: Annotation Ontology</b>

The Annotation Ontology is a vocabulary for performing several types of annotation  - comment, entities annotation (or semantic tags), textual annotation (classic tags), notes, examples, erratum... - on any kind of electronic document (text, images, audio, tables...) and document parts. AO is not providing any domain ontology but it is fostering the reuse of the existing ones for not breaking the principle of scalability of the Semantic Web.
